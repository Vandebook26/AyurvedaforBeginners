How Imbalances Happen (Vikruti)

Vikruti: Your Current State of Imbalance

Your current state of imbalance is called Vikruti. Ayurveda aims to bring your Vikruti back to your Prakriti, which is your natural balanced state.

In Ayurveda, Vikruti refers to the disturbance of the doshas from a person’s natural constitution. When doshas become aggravated or depleted, they can create discomfort, symptoms, and eventually disease if left uncorrected.

Common Causes of Imbalance

  • Diet: Eating foods that are too heavy, oily, spicy, or incompatible with your constitution.
  • Stress: Emotional and mental stress disturbs doshas, especially Vata and Pitta.
  • Weather and Seasons: Excess heat, cold, wind, or humidity can aggravate specific doshas.
  • Lifestyle: Irregular sleep, overwork, lack of exercise, or inactivity.

Signs of Dosha Imbalance

Dosha Common Signs of Imbalance
Vata (Air + Space) Dry skin, constipation, anxiety, restlessness, irregular appetite
Pitta (Fire + Water) Acid reflux, inflammation, irritability, excessive sweating, skin rashes
Kapha (Earth + Water) Weight gain, lethargy, congestion, slow digestion, heaviness or sleepiness

Early Detection

  • Ayurveda emphasizes noticing small changes in body and mind before disease develops.
  • Observing energy, digestion, sleep, mood, and elimination helps detect imbalance early.

Key idea: Understanding your dosha imbalance (Vikruti) helps you correct it through diet, lifestyle, and simple remedies before it turns into disease.
