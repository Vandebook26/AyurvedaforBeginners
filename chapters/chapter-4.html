Understanding Your Prakriti (Constitution)

Prakriti and Vikruti

Ayurveda explains health through two important concepts: Prakriti and Vikruti. Prakriti refers to your natural constitution, while Vikruti refers to the current imbalance in your body.

Understanding the difference between these two helps explain why each person responds differently to food, climate, stress, and lifestyle.

Prakriti: Your Natural Constitution

Every person is born with a unique combination of the three doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha. This natural balance is determined at the time of conception and remains the foundation of your physical and mental characteristics throughout life. This inherent constitution is called Prakriti.

Your Prakriti influences your body structure, digestion, metabolism, emotional tendencies, and how you react to environmental changes.

Some people have one dominant dosha, although this is relatively rare. Most individuals are dual types such as Vata-Pitta, Pitta-Kapha, or Vata-Kapha.

Knowing your Prakriti helps explain:

  • Why a diet that works well for someone else may not suit you.
  • Why certain climates or seasons feel comfortable to you while others do not.
  • Why people think, behave, and respond to stress differently.

Role of Prakriti in Health and Disease

Understanding a person's Prakriti allows Ayurveda to provide personalized guidance for diet, lifestyle, and therapies. When you live in alignment with your constitution, balance is maintained and health is preserved.

However, when lifestyle, food choices, or environmental influences disturb this balance, doshas become aggravated. This disturbance is known as Vikruti, which represents the current imbalance in the body.

Ayurvedic treatment focuses on restoring harmony between the doshas and bringing the body back toward its natural Prakriti.

Why Knowing Your Prakriti Matters

Understanding your Prakriti is like learning the language of your body. It helps you choose foods, exercises, and daily routines that support balance rather than work against your natural tendencies.

When you live according to your constitution, digestion improves, energy levels become stable, the mind remains calm, and sleep becomes more restful. These are classic signs of balanced health in Ayurveda.

Typical Characteristics of the Three Body Types

  • Vata Prakriti: Thin frame, dry skin, quick mind, variable digestion.
  • Pitta Prakriti: Medium build, warm body temperature, strong digestion, focused mind.
  • Kapha Prakriti: Sturdy build, smooth skin, slower metabolism, calm and steady personality.
